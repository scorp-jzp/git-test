Japchae

stir-fried glass noodles

Japchae is a sweet and savory dish of stir-fried glass noodles and vegetables that is popular in Korean cuisine. It is typically made with sweet potato starch noodles, known as dangmyeon, which are stir-fried with various vegetables, meat, and a sweet and savory sauce made from soy sauce and sugar. Japchae is often served as a side dish or as a main course, and is a popular dish for special occasions and holidays.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 pound beef, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 carrot, julienned
  • 4 ounces spinach, blanched and squeezed dry
  • 4 ounces dangmyeon (sweet potato starch noodles)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds

Instructions

  1. Marinate the beef with soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, black pepper, and garlic for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat a large pan over medium-high heat and add the marinated beef. Cook until the beef is browned and cooked through, then remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add the onion, bell peppers, and carrot. Cook until the vegetables are tender, then remove from the pan and set aside.
  4. Cook the spinach in the same pan until wilted, then remove from the pan and set aside.
  5. Cook the dangmyeon according to the package instructions, then drain and rinse under cold water.
  6. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, sugar, and sesame oil for the sauce.
  7. Heat the vegetable oil in the pan over medium heat, then add the cooked dangmyeon and the sauce. Cook, stirring constantly, until the noodles are well coated with the sauce.
  8. Add the cooked beef, vegetables, and spinach to the pan with the noodles. Cook, stirring constantly, until everything is well combined and heated through.
  9. Remove from heat and sprinkle with sesame seeds before serving.
